# 2023 Italian GP Qualifying

## VER vs SAI | Monza | 0.066s

### where SAI gains

The delta trace shows SAI recovering time in two clusters: the chicane sequences at Roggia and Ascari, and Parabolica entry around 4350m. The SF-23's mechanical grip and brake balance let Sainz carry more speed into the slow chicanes and rotate faster through them.

### where VER gains

VER builds his gap through the Lesmo complex (1400-1700m). The RB19's downforce-to-drag ratio translates directly into higher minimum cornering speeds. The lower drag also compounds across Monza's three long straights as a persistent 2-4 km/h surplus.

### the net picture

These advantages nearly cancel. The 0.066s qualifying gap is the residual of much larger spatial gains in opposite directions. A lap time hides all of this.

### what the numbers don't show

Verstappen's Lesmo advantage is not only aerodynamic. Trail-braking into a blind medium-speed corner at 210+ km/h is a commitment the driver makes every lap. The car enables it, the driver has to actually do it.

Sainz's braking zone gains are similar. Braking 2 metres later at 340 km/h is a margin that lives in muscle memory and confidence in the car's front-end response. The telemetry shows he found it repeatedly.

The spatial map shows where the time lives. It does not show what it costs to go get it.
